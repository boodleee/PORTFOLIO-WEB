Sustainable Development Goal 6

Clean Water and Sanitation

The United Nations Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) aim to address global challenges and improve the world for future generations. SDG 6 focuses on Clean Water and Sanitation, emphasizing the importance of universal access to clean water and sustainable sanitation practices. This goal is crucial for public health, economic development, and environmental sustainability, but achieving it requires collective global efforts.

What is the SDG 6?

SDG 6, titled "Clean Water and Sanitation," seeks to ensure that everyone, everywhere, has access to safe drinking water and adequate sanitation. By 2030, the goal is to achieve universal and equitable access to these basic services, which are vital to human well-being. The target also emphasizes the need for better water management, improved wastewater treatment, and reducing pollution to safeguard ecosystems.Pollution alert!

Key Target of SDG 6:

  • Universal access to clean water.
  • End open defecation and achieve sanitation for all.
  • Improve water quality by reducing pollution.
  • Support local communities in water management.
  • Protect and restore water-related ecosystems.
